## Purpose

Phoenix is built as a continuously evolving platform, not a short-lived application.

## Core Philosophy

We prefer longevity over haste, systems over isolated features, clarity over cleverness, evidence over assumptions, ownership over ambiguity, resilience over optimism, replaceability over lock-in, and user trust over vanity metrics.

## Non-Negotiable Rules

- No hidden business logic.
- No unowned data.
- No critical dependency without a replacement path.
- No material decision without documentation.
- No financial state without auditability.
- No AI capability without evaluation and governance.

## Architectural Integrity Check

- Does this preserve domain boundaries?
- Is ownership explicit?
- Is failure behavior documented?
- Can the decision evolve safely?
- Does this make Phoenix stronger in ten years?
