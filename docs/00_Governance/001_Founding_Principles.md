# GOV-002 — Founding Principles

**Document ID:** GOV-002  
**Knowledge ID:** KN-GOV-002  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Ratified  
**Owner:** Project Governance

## 1. Quality Before Speed

Phoenix shall not sacrifice long-term integrity for short-term delivery.

## 2. Clarity Before Cleverness

Simple and explainable designs are preferred over impressive but fragile complexity.

## 3. Evidence Before Assumptions

Important decisions require evidence, not optimism.

## 4. Replaceability Before Lock-In

Critical providers and infrastructure must be isolated behind Phoenix-owned contracts and adapters.

## 5. Failure Is Expected

Every important dependency will fail at some point. Phoenix must degrade gracefully and recover deliberately.

## 6. Evolution Is Continuous

No architecture is final. The system must evolve without uncontrolled rewrites.

## 7. Documentation Is Operational Memory

A decision that is not documented is not safely inherited.

## 8. Security and Privacy by Design

Security and privacy are architecture requirements, not launch checklists.

## 9. One Source of Truth

Each business fact has one authoritative owner and source.

## 10. Domain Ownership

Each business capability belongs to a bounded context with clear responsibility.

## 11. Financial Integrity

Every value-bearing event is traceable, idempotent, reconcilable, and auditable.

## 12. AI Accountability

AI systems must be versioned, evaluated, monitored, explainable where material, and subject to human oversight.

## 13. Global Readiness

Localization, cultural adaptation, time zones, currencies, and regional controls are designed from the beginning.

## 14. Operational Control

Phoenix must be able to observe, disable, replace, recover, and migrate critical components.

## 15. Sustainable Complexity

Complexity is accepted only when its benefit is measurable and its operation is understood.

## 16. Future Review Trigger

Review these principles when a proposed decision cannot be resolved by existing governance.
